About Me

I'm a Computer Engineering graduate student at NC State University with a strong foundation in digital hardware design. My work spans RTL development in SystemVerilog and Verilog, processor microarchitecture simulation, and memory system design.

Before graduate school I completed my Bachelor of Engineering in Electronics and Communication Engineering at Loyola ICAM College of Engineering and Technology (Anna University), where I also published research on IoT-based fire detection.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of hardware and software — from designing synthesisable RTL accelerators to simulating the micro-architecture of modern out-of-order processors.

Sep – Dec 2025

CNN Hardware Accelerator

RTL accelerator implementing 4×4 convolution, Leaky ReLU, and 2×2 average pooling for 1024×1024 signed 8-bit images. FSM-controlled with burst DRAM I/O and a line-buffer sliding window keeping only 5 rows on-chip. Synthesised with Synopsys DC — timing met with 1.90 ns positive slack.

  • 13-state FSM with burst DRAM read/write
  • 4-cycle multi-phase MAC unit
  • 185,670 cells · 368,793 µm² total area
  • 24 ns clock · critical path 21.57 ns
